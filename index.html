Who We Are

About Perstrix

STEM Racing (formerly F1 in Schools) is a student-led team of 3–6 people that competes in the F1 in Schools Development Class. We bring together engineering, design, and business skills to build high-performance miniature F1 cars. Along the way, we develop key abilities in STEM and enterprise.

Our name, Perstrix, originates from a blend of Peregrine Falcon and Stryx (Latin for owl) — two birds known for their unmatched speed, precision, and power. The Peregrine Falcon symbolises our pursuit of extreme performance and aerodynamic excellence, while Stryx represents intelligence and strategy. Together, they reflect the perfect balance between engineering skill and calculated design decisions.

"Ignorance is NOT bliss — at Team Perstrix we pay attention to the little details and leave no stone unturned. Through thorough analysis and study along with ideal sportsmanship we hope to succeed and advance."

Design Philosophy
01
Form Follows Function
Every surface, cut, and curve on our car serves a measurable aerodynamic purpose. We never add geometry for aesthetics alone — performance dictates design.
02
System-Level Aerodynamics
Rather than optimising individual components, we designed the car as a complete aerodynamic system — ensuring airflow interactions between parts work in harmony.
03
Iterative CFD Validation
Each design iteration was tested in ANSYS Discovery CFD before advancing to manufacture — eliminating guesswork and grounding every decision in simulation data.
04
Precision Manufacturing
Our car was produced using CNC routing and 3D printing to achieve tight tolerances, ensuring the manufactured part faithfully matches the optimised CAD model.
